Lok Sabha Election 2024 Results Analysis

Project Overview

This project analyzes the results of the 2024 Lok Sabha elections in India. The analysis is based on data sourced from the Election Commission of India’s official results page. The report provides a comprehensive overview of the overall results, state-wise highlights, and notable constituency outcomes.

Overall Election Results

  • Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP): 240 seats
  • Indian National Congress (INC): 99 seats
  • Samajwadi Party (SP): 37 seats
  • All India Trinamool Congress (AITC): 29 seats
  • Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK): 22 seats
  • Others: Remaining seats shared by various parties and independents

State-Wise Highlights

NCT of Delhi

  • BJP won all 7 seats.
  • Notable Constituencies: Chandni Chowk, East Delhi, New Delhi, North-East Delhi, North-West Delhi, South Delhi, West Delhi.

Tamil Nadu

  • DMK: 22 seats
  • INC: 9 seats
  • VCK: 2 seats
  • CPI: 2 seats
  • CPI(M): 2 seats
  • MDMK: 1 seat
  • IUML: 1 seat
  • Notable Constituencies: Chennai Central, Chennai North, Coimbatore, Kanyakumari, Madurai.

Punjab

  • INC: 7 seats
  • AAP: 3 seats
  • SAD: 1 seat
  • Independents: 2 seats
  • Notable Constituencies: Amritsar, Ludhiana, Patiala, Jalandhar, Gurdaspur.

Notable Constituencies

Akola (Maharashtra)

  • BJP Candidate: Anup Sanjay Dhotre won with 38.96% of the vote.
  • INC Candidate: Abhay Kashinath Patil secured 35.5% of the vote.
  • VBA Candidate: Prakash Yashwant Ambedkar received 23.59% of the vote.

Conclusion

The 2024 Lok Sabha elections resulted in a significant victory for the BJP, securing 240 seats. The INC, despite being the second-largest party, trailed significantly with 99 seats. State-wise analyses indicate strong regional performances by local parties, such as the DMK in Tamil Nadu and the INC in Punjab. Detailed constituency-level results provide insights into voter preferences and regional political dynamics.

For further details, visit the Election Commission of India's official results page.
